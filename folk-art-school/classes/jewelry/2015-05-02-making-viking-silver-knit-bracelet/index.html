Making a Viking Silver Knit Bracelet

May 2, 2015, with Liz Bucheit

Instructor
Liz Bucheit
Sessions
May 2, 2015, 9:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m.
Level of Instruction
All levels. No metalworking experience is required.
Tutition
  • $64 Members
  • $84 Non-members
Materials Fee
There will be a materials fee of $125 - $150. Charm not included.
Materials

Tools and supplies will be provided by the instructor.

Experience the timeless journey of traditional craftsmanship and the beauty of personal adornment as you discover the ancient art of chain making! Explore this centuries-old looping technique and create a beautiful fine silver bracelet with a variety of clasps. You will leave class with a completed piece.

Think Mother’s Day!

About the Instructor

Liz Bucheit creates jewelry inspired by Scandinavian folklore and myth. A goldsmith for over 30 years, she holds a Master’s degree in metalworking and jewelry from the University of Iowa, Iowa City, and has trained in traditional jewelry and metalworking techniques in Ireland and Norway. Liz has won numerous competitions and exhibited in museums and cultural centers. Liz is an active speaker on the topic of Norwegian filigree work and conducts workshops and classes in jewelry design and fabrication. She owns and operates Crown Trout Jewelers in Lanesboro, Minnesota.
