Rococo with Gold Leaf Cartouche

July 27-31, 2015, with Pam Rucinski

Instructor
Pam Rucinski
Sessions
July 27-31, 2015, 9:00 a.m.–5:00 p.m.
Level of Instruction
All levels.
Tutition
  • $320 Members
  • $370 Non-members
Materials

The class will be taught in oils; acrylic painters must have knowledge of both their colors and mediums.

Inspired by a sled at the Trøndelag Sverresborg Folkemuseum during Vesterheim’s folk art tour to Norway in 2014, Pam has created a flowing rococo design with gold leaf cartouche for a tray. Painters of all styles will be comfortable with this design since there are elements of Telemark, Hallingdal, and Gudbrandsdal in the original sled. Pam will also explore how rococo influenced the early rosemalers. Gold leaf will be taught.

About the Instructor

Pam Rucinski has been rosemaling since 1974. She teaches all over the United States and has taught three times in Japan, as well as traveled to Russia for folk art studies. She was on Vesterheim’s folk art tour to Norway in 2014. She teaches a weekly European folk art class through the Wisconsin Technical College that encompasses her three loves: Rosemaling (all styles), Swedish Dalmalning, and Russian Zhostovo. She is currently working with the Wisconsin Arts Board as a mentor in their Folk Arts Apprenticeship Program. Pam has taught at Vesterheim several times.
